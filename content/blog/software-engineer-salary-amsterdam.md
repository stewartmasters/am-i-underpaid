Software engineers in Amsterdam earn competitive salaries by European standards, with pay varying significantly by seniority. This page breaks down salary ranges for junior, mid-level, and senior engineers based on available compensation data for Amsterdam and the Netherlands.

## Amsterdam Software Engineer Salary by Seniority

The table below summarises gross annual salary ranges in EUR across seniority levels. Amsterdam-specific data is available for mid-level and senior roles; junior figures reflect Netherlands-wide data from Eurostat SES-2022.

Junior: EUR 38,000 – 54,000 (median EUR 45,000) — Netherlands-wide, Eurostat SES-2022
Mid-level (Amsterdam): EUR 56,000 – 88,000 (median EUR 70,000) — Eurostat SES-2022
Senior (Amsterdam): EUR 88,000 – 135,000 (median EUR 108,000) — Levels.fyi 2024

All figures are gross annual compensation in EUR. Ranges reflect the minimum to maximum observed in each dataset.

## Junior Software Engineer Salary in Amsterdam

Junior software engineers in the Netherlands earn between EUR 38,000 and EUR 54,000 per year, with a median of EUR 45,000, according to Eurostat SES-2022 data. Amsterdam-specific figures for junior roles are not available in the current dataset. Given Amsterdam's status as the country's primary tech hub, entry-level salaries in the city may skew toward the upper end of the national range, but no city-level figure can be confirmed from available data.

## Mid-Level Software Engineer Salary in Amsterdam

Mid-level software engineers in Amsterdam earn between EUR 56,000 and EUR 88,000 gross per year, with a median of EUR 70,000, based on Eurostat SES-2022 data. This compares to a Netherlands-wide mid-level range of EUR 52,000 to EUR 81,000 (median EUR 64,000) from the same source, indicating an Amsterdam premium of roughly EUR 6,000 at the median. Engineers at this level typically have two to five years of professional experience.

## Senior Software Engineer Salary in Amsterdam

Senior software engineers in Amsterdam see the widest salary range in the dataset. Levels.fyi 2024 data places the range at EUR 88,000 to EUR 135,000, with a median of EUR 108,000. Eurostat SES-2022 data for senior engineers at the Netherlands-wide level shows a range of EUR 72,000 to EUR 110,000 (median EUR 88,000). The gap between the two sources likely reflects differences in methodology: Levels.fyi skews toward tech-company compensation, while Eurostat covers a broader employer base.

## Key Factors That Affect Your Salary

Several variables influence where an individual engineer falls within these ranges. Company type is a major driver: product companies and international tech firms in Amsterdam typically pay above the national median, while agencies and public-sector employers tend to pay closer to the floor. Specialisation also matters — data not available for specific stack or domain premiums in this dataset. Other factors include language skills (English proficiency is standard; Dutch can add value in client-facing roles), equity or bonus components, and years of experience within a seniority band.

## Data Sources and Confidence Notes

The figures on this page are drawn from two sources: Eurostat Structure of Earnings Survey (SES-2022) and Levels.fyi (2024 manual seed). Confidence scores in the underlying data range from 0.31 to 0.45 on a 0–1 scale, indicating moderate uncertainty. Eurostat SES data is broad-based and statistically robust but may lag current market conditions. Levels.fyi data reflects self-reported compensation, which can over-represent larger tech employers. Use these ranges as a benchmark, not a guarantee. All figures are gross annual salary in EUR before tax and social contributions.
