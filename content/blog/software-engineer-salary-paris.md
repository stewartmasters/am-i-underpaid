Software engineer salaries in Paris vary significantly by seniority and employer type. This page breaks down compensation ranges for junior, mid-level, and senior engineers in Paris, drawing on multiple data sources to give you a clear benchmark for your role and experience level.

## Paris Salary Ranges at a Glance

Based on available 2024 data, mid-level software engineers in Paris earn between EUR 44,000 and EUR 70,000, with a median of EUR 55,000 (Eurostat SES). Senior engineers see a wider range: EUR 62,000 to EUR 100,000, with a median of EUR 78,000. A second dataset from Levels.fyi reports higher figures for the same seniority bands, reflecting compensation at tech-focused and international employers — mid-level: EUR 50,000–78,000 (median EUR 62,000); senior: EUR 72,000–115,000 (median EUR 90,000). Data not available for junior-level salaries specific to Paris city.

## Mid-Level Software Engineer Salary in Paris

Mid-level engineers in Paris can expect a median gross annual salary of EUR 55,000 according to Eurostat SES data, with the range spanning EUR 44,000 at the lower end to EUR 70,000 at the upper end. Levels.fyi data, which skews toward larger tech employers, places the median higher at EUR 62,000, within a EUR 50,000–78,000 range. The gap between these two sources reflects the difference between the broader Paris job market and roles at tech-focused companies.

## Senior Software Engineer Salary in Paris

Senior software engineers command a notable premium in Paris. Eurostat SES data puts the median at EUR 78,000, with a range of EUR 62,000 to EUR 100,000. Levels.fyi data reports a higher median of EUR 90,000 and a ceiling of EUR 115,000, with a floor of EUR 72,000. The spread between sources is wider at the senior level, suggesting that employer type, specialisation, and negotiation play a larger role in determining total compensation as engineers advance.

## How Paris Compares to the Rest of France

Paris salaries sit meaningfully above the national average for software engineers. For mid-level roles, the France-wide Eurostat SES median is EUR 47,500 (range: EUR 38,000–60,000), compared to EUR 55,000 in Paris — a difference of EUR 7,500 at the median. For senior roles, the national median is EUR 68,000 versus EUR 78,000 in Paris. Junior engineers nationally earn a median of EUR 34,000 (range: EUR 28,000–40,000); Paris-specific junior data is not available in this dataset. The Paris premium reflects higher living costs, greater employer density, and stronger demand for tech talent in the capital.

## Understanding the Data Sources

This page draws on two sources. Eurostat Structure of Earnings Survey (SES) data covers a broad cross-section of employers across France, including both large and small companies; the Paris-specific figures use the SES-2022-FR dataset. Levels.fyi data is self-reported by professionals, with a bias toward larger, often international tech employers. Neither source captures total compensation including bonuses, equity, or benefits. Confidence scores for these datasets range from 0.31 to 0.45, indicating moderate reliability; treat these figures as directional benchmarks rather than precise guarantees.

## Using This Data to Assess Your Pay

To benchmark your own salary, start by identifying your seniority level and comparing your current gross annual salary to the median for your band. If you are a mid-level engineer earning below EUR 55,000 in Paris, you may have room to negotiate — particularly if your employer is a tech-focused company where Levels.fyi benchmarks apply. For senior engineers, the EUR 78,000–90,000 median range provides a useful anchor. Keep in mind that factors such as tech stack, industry vertical, company size, and individual performance all influence where within a range a given offer falls. Data not available for staff, principal, or engineering manager levels.
