## Salary Ranges by Seniority Level (Germany)

The following ranges come from Destatis VSE 2022 survey data for software engineers across Germany. Junior level (entry to ~2 years): min EUR 35,000 — median EUR 41,500 — max EUR 48,000. Mid level (~3–6 years): min EUR 48,000 — median EUR 58,500 — max EUR 72,000. Senior level (7+ years): min EUR 68,000 — median EUR 80,000 — max EUR 100,000. A separate Eurostat SES 2022 dataset for mid-level engineers nationally shows a similar range: min EUR 47,000 — median EUR 58,000 — max EUR 73,000. The consistency across two independent national surveys increases confidence in the mid-level figures as a reasonable baseline.

## Berlin Comparison: A City-Level Reference Point

Because Hamburg city data is not available, Berlin provides the most relevant city-level comparison within Germany. Destatis VSE 2022 data for mid-level software engineers in Berlin shows: min EUR 46,000 — median EUR 56,000 — max EUR 70,000. A separate Levels.fyi 2024 dataset for mid-level engineers in Berlin reports a higher range: min EUR 55,000 — median EUR 72,000 — max EUR 90,000. The gap between these two sources likely reflects differences in methodology — Levels.fyi skews toward tech-company roles at larger employers, while Destatis VSE covers the broader economy. Hamburg's market is generally considered comparable to Berlin, though direct Hamburg data is needed for a precise comparison.

## Key Factors That Influence Pay in Hamburg

Several variables shift compensation above or below the median figures shown here. Company type: international tech firms and financial services companies in Hamburg typically pay above the national median, while mid-size German Mittelstand employers often sit closer to or below it. Tech stack and specialisation: roles requiring cloud infrastructure, machine learning, or embedded systems command premiums that are not captured in broad survey averages. Contract type: permanent employees and contractors have structurally different pay structures; the figures above cover employed positions only. Data not available for contractor rate benchmarks in Hamburg.

## How to Use These Benchmarks

Treat the median figures as a starting point, not a ceiling. If your total compensation — including bonus, equity, and benefits — falls materially below the national median for your seniority, that is a signal worth investigating before your next review or job search. When comparing offers, normalise for gross annual salary and check whether bonus is guaranteed or discretionary. Hamburg has a relatively high cost of living compared to many German cities, which some employers factor into offers.
