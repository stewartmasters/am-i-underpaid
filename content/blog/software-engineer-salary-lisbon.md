Software engineers in Lisbon earn above the Portuguese national average across all seniority levels. Based on INE Portugal survey data (2022), Lisbon median salaries range from EUR 21,000 for junior engineers to EUR 44,000 for senior engineers. Use these benchmarks to assess whether your current compensation is competitive.

## Lisbon Salary Benchmarks by Seniority

The table below summarises gross annual salary ranges for software engineers in Lisbon, Portugal. All figures are in EUR and sourced from INE Portugal (data version INE-PT-IEG-2022-PT).

Junior Software Engineer: Min EUR 17,000 — Median EUR 21,000 — Max EUR 27,000
Mid-Level Software Engineer: Min EUR 23,000 — Median EUR 30,000 — Max EUR 39,000
Senior Software Engineer: Min EUR 33,000 — Median EUR 44,000 — Max EUR 58,000

The median is the most reliable single reference point. If your salary falls below the minimum for your level, it may be worth benchmarking further or preparing a case for a pay review.

## What Counts as Junior, Mid, and Senior?

Seniority definitions vary by company, but the following general guidelines apply in the Portuguese tech market. Junior engineers typically have fewer than 2 years of professional experience and work under close supervision. Mid-level engineers generally have 2 to 5 years of experience and operate with moderate autonomy. Senior engineers usually have 5 or more years of experience, lead technical decisions, and may mentor others. If your title does not match your responsibilities, use the level that best reflects your actual scope of work when benchmarking.

## Understanding the Data: Source and Confidence

All salary figures on this page are drawn from the INE Portugal Inquérito à Estrutura de Ganhos (IEG), data version INE-PT-IEG-2022-PT. This is a statutory employer survey conducted by Statistics Portugal and is one of the most comprehensive compensation datasets available for the country. The confidence score for these records is 0.44, which reflects moderate statistical confidence — primarily because the dataset does not isolate Lisbon as a distinct stratum in all breakdowns. Treat the figures as directional benchmarks rather than precise guarantees. For the most current data, check whether a more recent INE survey cycle has been published.
