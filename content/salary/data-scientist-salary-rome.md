## No Rome-Specific Data: Why That Matters

Our current dataset does not include salary records specific to Rome or Italy. Italy's tech compensation landscape is generally less documented in public salary surveys than markets like Germany, the UK, or the Netherlands, which means professionals in Rome often have less benchmarking data to work with than peers in other major European cities.

## Mid-Level Data Scientist Salaries Across Europe

For mid-level data scientists, our dataset shows the following annual salary ranges in comparable European markets. In Berlin, the range runs from EUR 58,000 to EUR 90,000, with a median of EUR 72,000. In Amsterdam, the range is EUR 55,000 to EUR 92,000, with a median of EUR 72,000. In London, mid-level data scientists earn between GBP 65,000 and GBP 105,000, with a median of GBP 82,000. These figures give a useful reference frame for Southern European professionals, particularly when evaluating cross-border opportunities or remote roles. ## How Seniority Shifts Compensation

Seniority is one of the strongest drivers of compensation for data scientists across all European markets. Swiss national data — which reflects one of Europe's highest-paying markets — illustrates the scale of this progression clearly. Junior data scientists in Switzerland earn between CHF 72,000 and CHF 102,000 (median CHF 86,000). At the mid level, that range moves to CHF 94,000–CHF 134,000 (median CHF 112,000). Senior data scientists reach CHF 128,000–CHF 182,000, with a median of CHF 152,000. While Switzerland is not directly comparable to Rome, the seniority-driven progression pattern is consistent across European markets and is a key factor to account for when benchmarking your own compensation.

Without city-specific data for Rome, professionals there should weigh several factors when assessing their pay. Swiss national figures come from the BFS Labour Structure Survey (LSE-2022-CH), with a confidence score of 0.47. These confidence scores reflect the current volume and recency of underlying records.
