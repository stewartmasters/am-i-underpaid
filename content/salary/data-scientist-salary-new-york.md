If you're benchmarking data scientist salary New York figures for a job offer, a raise conversation, or a career move, understanding where you stand relative to the market is essential. This guide breaks down what the data shows — by seniority level and in comparison to other major tech hubs worldwide.

## Why New York Is a Benchmark Market for Data Scientists

New York City is one of the most competitive and high-paying markets for data science talent globally. The city's concentration of financial services, media, healthcare, and tech firms creates sustained demand for professionals who can work with large datasets and build predictive models. Compensation in NYC reflects both that demand and the city's high cost of living, making it a useful anchor when evaluating offers or negotiating pay.

## How New York Data Scientist Salaries Compare Globally

To put New York compensation in context, it helps to look at what data scientists earn in other major markets. In London, mid-level data scientists earn between £65,000 and £105,000, with a median of £82,000 (GBP). In Berlin, the mid-level range runs from €58,000 to €90,000, with a median of €72,000 (EUR). Amsterdam sits at a similar level, with mid-level salaries ranging from €55,000 to €92,000 and a median of €72,000 (EUR). Switzerland stands out at the senior level, where data scientists earn between CHF 128,000 and CHF 182,000, with a median of CHF 152,000. ## Seniority Levels and What They Mean for Pay

Across markets, seniority is the single biggest driver of compensation variance for data scientists. Using Switzerland as a reference point with strong survey coverage: junior data scientists see a salary range of CHF 72,000 to CHF 102,000 (median CHF 86,000), mid-level professionals range from CHF 94,000 to CHF 134,000 (median CHF 112,000), and senior data scientists command CHF 128,000 to CHF 182,000 (median CHF 152,000). The jump from junior to senior represents a substantial earnings uplift — a pattern that holds across most developed markets, including New York.

## Factors That Influence Your Data Scientist Salary

Beyond seniority, several variables shape where a data scientist lands within any given salary band. Industry sector is a major factor — financial services and tech firms typically pay at the top of the range, while non-profits and public sector roles tend to sit lower. Specialization matters too: expertise in machine learning engineering, NLP, or large-scale data infrastructure commands a premium over generalist analytics roles. Company size, equity compensation, and bonus structures can also significantly alter total compensation beyond base salary.
