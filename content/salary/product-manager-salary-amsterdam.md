Wondering what a Product Manager earns in Amsterdam? Based on Netherlands-wide compensation data, mid-level Product Managers earn a median of €70,000 per year, with a range spanning from €56,000 to €88,000. This page breaks down the figures and what they mean for your benchmarking.

## Salary Range for Mid-Level Product Managers in the Netherlands

According to Eurostat Structure of Earnings Survey data (SES-2022), mid-level Product Managers in the Netherlands fall within the following gross annual salary range:

Minimum: €56,000
Median: €70,000
Maximum: €88,000

These figures are denominated in EUR and represent gross annual compensation. The median of €70,000 is the most reliable single benchmark for a typical mid-career Product Manager in this market. The spread between the minimum and maximum reflects variation in company size, industry sector, and individual experience within the mid seniority band.

## Amsterdam vs. Netherlands-Wide Data

The salary records available for this page are sourced at the Netherlands country level, not specifically filtered to Amsterdam city. Data not available for Amsterdam-specific salary differentials. Amsterdam is the Netherlands' primary tech and business hub, and compensation in the city may differ from the national figure, but no city-level data is currently available in our dataset to quantify that gap. Use the national median of €70,000 as your baseline when benchmarking an Amsterdam-based role.

## How to Use This Data for Salary Negotiations

The median salary figure is the most actionable benchmark. If your current or offered gross annual salary is below €70,000 for a mid-level Product Manager role in the Netherlands, you have a data-backed basis to negotiate upward. If you are above the €88,000 upper bound, you are likely compensated above the typical market range for this seniority level, which may reflect specialised skills, a high-growth company, or additional responsibilities. Always factor in total compensation — including bonuses, equity, and benefits — when assessing your overall package. Data not available for bonus or equity benchmarks for this role and location.

## Data Sources and Confidence Notes

The salary figures on this page are drawn from the Eurostat Structure of Earnings Survey (SES-2022 and SES-2022-NL), which is a large-scale, harmonised employer survey conducted across EU member states. Two records are available for this role and country combination. Confidence scores for these records are 0.48 and 0.37 respectively on a 0–1 scale, indicating moderate-to-low statistical confidence. This is partly due to the data being aggregated at the national level rather than city level. Treat these figures as a solid directional benchmark rather than a precise market rate.
