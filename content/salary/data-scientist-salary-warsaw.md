## European Benchmark: Mid-Level Data Scientist Salaries

To contextualise Warsaw compensation, it helps to look at verified mid-level data scientist salary ranges across comparable European cities. In Berlin, mid-level data scientists earn between EUR 58,000 and EUR 90,000, with a median of EUR 72,000. In Amsterdam, the range is EUR 55,000 to EUR 92,000, with a median of EUR 72,000. In London, the range is GBP 65,000 to GBP 105,000, with a median of GBP 82,000. These figures come from the levels-fyi 2024 dataset. Warsaw salaries typically sit below these Western European figures in gross terms, though the gap narrows when adjusted for local purchasing power. ## Senior and Junior Ranges: The Swiss Reference Point

Switzerland represents the top end of the European data science pay scale. According to the Swiss Federal Statistical Office Labour Structure Survey (LSE 2022), junior data scientists in Switzerland earn between CHF 72,000 and CHF 102,000 (median CHF 86,000), mid-level professionals earn CHF 94,000 to CHF 134,000 (median CHF 112,000), and senior data scientists earn CHF 128,000 to CHF 182,000 (median CHF 152,000). These figures carry a confidence score of 0.47, indicating moderate data density. They serve as a ceiling benchmark for European compensation rather than a direct Warsaw comparator.

## Key Factors That Shape Data Scientist Pay in Warsaw

Several variables drive compensation differences within Warsaw's data science market. Industry sector matters significantly — financial services, e-commerce, and enterprise software firms tend to pay above the local average. Company size and ownership structure (multinational vs. domestic) also play a role, with multinationals often aligning pay closer to Western European scales. Seniority level, specialisation (machine learning engineering, NLP, computer vision), and proficiency in English all influence total compensation. Equity and bonus structures vary widely and can meaningfully affect total annual earnings.
