## Salary Ranges by Seniority Level

All figures are in CHF (gross annual) and sourced from the Swiss Federal Statistical Office Labor Structure Survey (LSE-2022-CH). Note that these are national Switzerland figures; city-level breakdowns for Zurich specifically are not available in the current dataset.

Junior Data Scientist: CHF 72,000 min — CHF 86,000 median — CHF 102,000 max

Mid-Level Data Scientist: CHF 94,000 min — CHF 112,000 median — CHF 134,000 max

Senior Data Scientist: CHF 128,000 min — CHF 152,000 median — CHF 182,000 max

The spread between junior median (CHF 86,000) and senior median (CHF 152,000) reflects a 77% uplift over a typical career progression. The wide max ranges at senior level indicate that top earners in finance, pharma, and tech sectors can command significantly above-median compensation.

## What Drives Pay Variation in Zurich

Zurich concentrates Switzerland's largest financial institutions, global tech offices, and life sciences firms — all heavy consumers of data science talent. Several factors push individual salaries above or below the national median.

Industry: Banking and asset management roles typically carry higher base salaries than public sector or NGO positions. Data not available for industry-specific breakdowns in this dataset.

Skill stack: Proficiency in machine learning engineering, MLOps, or domain-specific modeling (e.g., quantitative finance, genomics) commands a premium over general analytics roles.

Language: Zurich operates primarily in German and English. Fluency in both broadens the accessible employer pool.

Company size: Data not available for employer-size segmentation in this dataset.

## How to Read These Figures

The min/max values represent the typical range observed in the survey, not absolute floor or ceiling. A junior professional at the lower end of the range may be in their first role or transitioning from a related field. A senior professional at the upper end likely combines deep technical expertise with team leadership or client-facing responsibilities.

The confidence score for this dataset is 0.47, which reflects a moderate sample size at the role-and-seniority level. Treat the median as a reliable anchor and the min/max as directional boundaries rather than hard limits.

Data version: LSE-2022-CH. Figures may not reflect salary movements that occurred after 2022.
