## How Seniority Shifts the Range

Seniority is one of the strongest drivers of compensation variance for data scientists across Europe. Swiss national data from the BFS Labour Structure Survey (LSE 2022) illustrates the progression clearly: junior data scientists earn between CHF 72,000 and CHF 102,000 (median CHF 86,000), mid-level professionals earn CHF 94,000 to CHF 134,000 (median CHF 112,000), and senior data scientists command CHF 128,000 to CHF 182,000 (median CHF 152,000). While Switzerland is a distinct market, this seniority gradient is broadly consistent across Western Europe and is a useful structural reference.

## Key Factors That Influence Your Offer in Brussels

Beyond city and seniority, several factors shape where an individual offer lands within any salary band. Industry sector matters significantly — financial services, consulting, and EU institutions in Brussels tend to offer different compensation structures than startups or scale-ups. Proficiency in specific tools (Python, SQL, ML frameworks), the scope of the role (research-focused vs. production-focused), and whether the position includes equity or performance bonuses all shift the effective total compensation. Evaluating an offer on base salary alone can be misleading.
