San Francisco remains one of the most competitive markets for data science talent in the world. If you are researching data scientist salary San Francisco figures to benchmark your compensation or evaluate a job offer, this guide breaks down what the data shows — by seniority level and in comparison to other major tech cities globally.

## Why San Francisco Is a Benchmark Market for Data Scientists

San Francisco and the broader Bay Area have long set the ceiling for data science compensation globally. The concentration of major technology employers, venture-backed startups, and AI-focused firms creates sustained upward pressure on salaries. Understanding where you sit relative to the market requires looking at seniority level, total compensation structure, and how the city compares to international peers.

## How San Francisco Compares to Global Data Science Salaries

To put San Francisco compensation in context, it helps to look at what data scientists earn in other major markets. In London, mid-level data scientists earn between £65,000 and £105,000, with a median of £82,000 (GBP). In Amsterdam, the mid-level range runs from €55,000 to €92,000, with a median of €72,000 (EUR). Berlin sits at a similar range, with mid-level salaries spanning €58,000 to €90,000 and a median of €72,000 (EUR). Switzerland stands out among European markets: mid-level data scientists there earn between CHF 94,000 and CHF 134,000, with a median of CHF 112,000. For more context on European benchmarks, see the [Data Scientist Salary in London (2024)](https://salaryverdict.com/blog/data-scientist-salary-london) guide.

## Seniority Levels and What They Mean for Pay

Seniority is one of the strongest drivers of compensation variance in data science. Using Switzerland as a high-paying European reference point: junior data scientists earn a median of CHF 86,000 (range: CHF 72,000–102,000), mid-level professionals earn a median of CHF 112,000 (range: CHF 94,000–134,000), and senior data scientists earn a median of CHF 152,000 (range: CHF 128,000–182,000). The jump from junior to senior represents a substantial increase in earning potential, a pattern consistent across all major data science markets. Defining your seniority level accurately is critical before benchmarking your salary.

## Key Factors That Influence Your Data Science Salary

Beyond seniority, several factors shape where an individual falls within a salary band. Industry vertical matters — financial services, healthcare technology, and enterprise software companies often pay differently than consumer tech or media. Specialization in machine learning engineering, NLP, or computer vision can command a premium over generalist data science roles. Company stage (public vs. private, startup vs. enterprise) also affects base salary versus equity trade-offs, which are especially significant in San Francisco's startup-heavy ecosystem.
