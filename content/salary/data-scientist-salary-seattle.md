## Why Seattle Is a Key Market for Data Scientists

Seattle is home to some of the world's largest technology employers, making it one of the most competitive markets for data science talent in North America. The concentration of major tech companies drives strong demand for data scientists at all levels, from entry-level analysts to senior machine learning specialists. Understanding where you sit within the pay spectrum requires looking at seniority bands, not just a single average figure.

## Data Scientist Pay by Seniority: A Global Reference Point

While Seattle-specific figures are not included in our current dataset, global benchmarks from comparable high-cost tech markets provide useful context. In Switzerland, which ranks among the highest-paying markets for data scientists worldwide, junior data scientists earn between CHF 72,000 and CHF 102,000, with a median of CHF 86,000. Mid-level professionals see a range of CHF 94,000 to CHF 134,000 (median CHF 112,000), while senior data scientists command CHF 128,000 to CHF 182,000, with a median of CHF 152,000. These figures are sourced from the Swiss Federal Statistical Office (LSE-2022-CH) and illustrate how sharply compensation scales with seniority in premium tech markets — a pattern that holds across Seattle as well.

## How Seattle Compares to European Tech Hubs

For additional context, mid-level data scientists in London earn between GBP 65,000 and GBP 105,000, with a median of GBP 82,000. ## Key Factors That Drive Your Data Scientist Salary

Across every market, a handful of variables consistently move the needle on data scientist compensation. Seniority is the most significant: the Swiss data shows senior pay at nearly double junior pay at the median. Beyond level, specialization matters — professionals with expertise in machine learning engineering, NLP, or large-scale data infrastructure tend to command premiums. Company size and funding stage also play a role, with late-stage tech firms and public companies generally offering higher total compensation packages than early-stage startups.
