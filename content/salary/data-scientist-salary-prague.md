## Seniority and Salary Progression

Seniority has a significant impact on data scientist compensation across Europe. Swiss national data from the BFS Labour Structure Survey (LSE-2022) illustrates this progression clearly: junior data scientists earn between CHF 72,000 and CHF 102,000 (median CHF 86,000), mid-level professionals earn CHF 94,000 to CHF 134,000 (median CHF 112,000), and senior data scientists earn CHF 128,000 to CHF 182,000 (median CHF 152,000). While Switzerland represents a high-cost, high-compensation market, the relative step-up between seniority bands is a useful structural benchmark.

Prague is widely regarded as one of Central Europe's leading tech hubs, with a growing presence of international tech firms and data-focused teams. Cost of living in Prague is notably lower than in Berlin, Amsterdam, or London, which means that gross salary figures tend to be lower while purchasing power can remain competitive. ## Key Factors That Influence Your Data Scientist Salary

Regardless of city, several variables consistently drive compensation outcomes for data scientists. Seniority level is the single largest factor, as the European data above demonstrates. Industry sector matters too — finance, pharma, and enterprise tech tend to pay above market rates. Specialisation in areas such as machine learning engineering, NLP, or MLOps typically commands a premium over generalist data science roles. Company size and funding stage also play a role, with well-funded scale-ups and multinationals generally offering stronger total compensation packages than early-stage startups.
