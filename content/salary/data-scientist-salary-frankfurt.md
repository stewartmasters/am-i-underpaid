If you are researching data scientist salary Frankfurt figures to benchmark your pay or evaluate a job offer, this guide breaks down what the data shows for Germany's financial capital and how it fits into the broader European compensation landscape.

## What Data Scientists Earn in Germany: The Benchmark

Direct Frankfurt-specific salary records are limited in current survey data, but German market benchmarks provide a strong reference point. For a mid-level Data Scientist in Germany, salary data from levels.fyi points to a median of €72,000 per year, with a range spanning €58,000 at the lower end to €90,000 at the upper end. These figures reflect total gross annual compensation in euros and are based on 2024 survey data. Frankfurt, as one of Germany's highest cost-of-living cities and a major financial hub, typically commands compensation at or above the national mid-market median.

## Seniority and Its Impact on Pay

The €58,000–€90,000 range captured in current data reflects mid-level Data Scientist roles. In practice, the spread within that band is driven by factors such as years of experience, technical specialisation (machine learning engineering, NLP, computer vision), and the size and sector of the employer. Frankfurt's concentration of financial services firms, including major banks and asset managers, means demand for data scientists with quantitative finance or risk modelling backgrounds can push compensation toward the higher end of the mid-level range.

## Key Factors That Influence Your Frankfurt Salary

Several variables determine where an individual offer lands within the market range. Industry sector is significant: financial services, consulting, and enterprise software firms in Frankfurt typically pay more than public sector or non-profit organisations. Company size matters too, with larger multinationals generally offering higher base salaries and structured bonus schemes. Technical stack proficiency — particularly in Python, SQL, and cloud platforms — remains a consistent differentiator in compensation negotiations. Holding a relevant advanced degree (MSc or PhD in statistics, computer science, or a quantitative field) also correlates with higher starting offers at the mid level.
