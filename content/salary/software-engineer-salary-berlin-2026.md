Software engineer salaries in Berlin vary significantly by seniority, employer type, and data source. This page compiles figures from multiple compensation surveys to give you a grounded view of what the market looks like — so you can benchmark your own pay with confidence.

## Berlin Mid-Level Software Engineer Salary

Berlin-specific data for mid-level software engineers points to a salary range of EUR 46,000 to EUR 90,000, depending on the source. The Destatis VSE survey (VSE-2022) places the Berlin mid-level median at EUR 56,000, with a range of EUR 46,000 to EUR 70,000. The Levels.fyi dataset, which skews toward tech-focused and international employers, shows a higher median of EUR 72,000 and a range of EUR 55,000 to EUR 90,000. The gap between these two sources reflects differences in company type and reporting methodology rather than a single definitive market rate.

## Germany-Wide Benchmarks by Seniority

For broader context, national-level data from Destatis VSE (VSE-2022) and Eurostat SES (SES-2022) covers three seniority bands across Germany. Junior software engineers: median EUR 41,500, range EUR 35,000 to EUR 48,000 (Destatis VSE). Mid-level software engineers: median EUR 58,000 to EUR 58,500, range EUR 47,000 to EUR 73,000 (Eurostat SES and Destatis VSE respectively). Senior software engineers: median EUR 80,000, range EUR 68,000 to EUR 100,000 (Destatis VSE). Berlin salaries for mid-level roles tend to sit slightly below the national mid-level median in traditional survey data, though tech-sector employers in Berlin often pay above that baseline.

## How Berlin Compares to the National Average

Comparing Berlin-specific figures to Germany-wide data reveals a nuanced picture. The Destatis VSE Berlin mid-level median of EUR 56,000 is modestly below the national mid-level median of EUR 58,500 from the same source. This is consistent with Berlin's position as a startup and scale-up hub rather than a concentration of large enterprise or financial-sector employers, which tend to anchor higher pay in cities like Munich or Frankfurt. Data not available for a direct city-by-city comparison across all major German metros.

## Understanding the Data Sources

The figures on this page come from three distinct sources, each with different methodologies and coverage. Levels.fyi collects self-reported compensation data, with a strong skew toward software engineers at tech companies and multinationals — confidence score 0.45. Eurostat SES (Structure of Earnings Survey, 2022) is a large-scale employer survey covering broad industry sectors across EU member states — confidence score 0.39. Destatis VSE (Verdienststrukturerhebung, 2022) is the German federal statistical office's earnings structure survey, offering nationally representative data — confidence score 0.33. No single source captures the full market. Cross-referencing multiple sources gives a more reliable picture than relying on any one dataset.

## What Affects Your Salary as a Software Engineer in Berlin

Beyond seniority, several factors influence where an individual salary lands within these ranges. Company stage and type matter significantly: established tech firms and international companies typically pay toward the upper end of published ranges, while early-stage startups may offer lower base salaries alongside equity. Specialisation in areas such as machine learning, cloud infrastructure, or security can command a premium. Data not available for compensation breakdowns by specific technical stack or industry vertical within Berlin. Negotiation, total compensation structure (including bonuses and equity), and whether a role is remote-eligible also affect effective pay.
