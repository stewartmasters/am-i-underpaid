Frankfurt is one of Germany's most economically significant cities, home to major financial institutions and a growing tech sector. This page compiles salary benchmark data for software engineers in Germany to help you assess where your compensation stands. Note: Frankfurt-specific city-level salary records are not available in our current dataset; figures below draw on Germany-wide and Berlin-city benchmarks from Eurostat SES 2022, Destatis VSE 2022, and Levels.fyi 2024.

## Mid-Level Software Engineer Salary in Germany

Multiple sources converge on a similar range for mid-level software engineers across Germany. According to Eurostat SES 2022, the national median sits at EUR 58,000, with a range of EUR 47,000 to EUR 73,000. Destatis VSE 2022 data aligns closely, showing a median of EUR 58,500 and a range of EUR 48,000 to EUR 72,000. These figures represent the broadest national baseline and are the most relevant reference point for Frankfurt in the absence of Frankfurt-specific data.

## Junior and Senior Software Engineer Salary Benchmarks

Seniority has a significant impact on total compensation. For junior software engineers in Germany, Destatis VSE 2022 data shows a salary range of EUR 35,000 to EUR 48,000, with a median of EUR 41,500. At the senior level, the same source reports a range of EUR 68,000 to EUR 100,000 and a median of EUR 80,000. The jump from junior to senior median represents a difference of approximately EUR 38,500 per year, underscoring the value of experience and progression in this field.

## Berlin as a City-Level Reference Point

No Frankfurt-specific city-level records are currently available in our dataset. Berlin data provides the closest available city-level comparison. Destatis VSE 2022 places the mid-level Berlin software engineer median at EUR 56,000, within a range of EUR 46,000 to EUR 70,000. Levels.fyi 2024 data for Berlin shows a higher median of EUR 72,000, ranging from EUR 55,000 to EUR 90,000, likely reflecting a concentration of higher-paying tech and startup roles in that sample. Frankfurt, as a major financial hub, may skew toward the higher end of national ranges, but city-specific data is not available to confirm this.

## How to Use These Benchmarks

These figures are drawn from multiple sources with varying methodologies and confidence levels. Eurostat SES and Destatis VSE are large-scale official surveys covering broad employer populations, while Levels.fyi reflects self-reported data from tech-focused professionals and tends to capture higher-paying roles. When benchmarking your own salary, consider which source most closely matches your employer type, company size, and role scope. Use the national median as a floor and the Berlin city-level data as a directional indicator for major urban markets like Frankfurt.

## Data Limitations and Confidence Notes

All records in this dataset carry moderate-to-low confidence scores, ranging from 0.33 to 0.45 on a 0 to 1 scale. Frankfurt-specific salary data is not available for this role. The national Germany figures from Destatis and Eurostat are the most statistically robust given their large sample sizes, but they cover survey periods ending in 2022 and may not fully reflect current market conditions.
