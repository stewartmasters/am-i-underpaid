## How Dublin Compares to Other European Cities

While Dublin-specific figures are not available in our current dataset, we can provide a snapshot of mid-level data scientist salaries across comparable European cities to give you a directional benchmark.

In London, mid-level data scientists earn between £65,000 and £105,000, with a median of £82,000 GBP (source: levels-fyi, 2024). In Amsterdam, the equivalent range is €55,000 to €92,000, with a median of €72,000 EUR. Berlin mid-level data scientists see a range of €58,000 to €90,000, with a median of €72,000 EUR. In Switzerland (national level), mid-level data scientists earn between CHF 94,000 and CHF 134,000, with a median of CHF 112,000 (source: BFS-LSE, 2022).

## Seniority Levels and What They Mean for Pay

Across European markets where data is available, seniority has a significant impact on data scientist compensation. Using Switzerland as an illustrative example: junior data scientists earn a median of CHF 86,000, mid-level professionals earn a median of CHF 112,000, and senior data scientists reach a median of CHF 152,000 — with a ceiling of CHF 182,000 at the top of the range. This pattern of meaningful step-ups between seniority bands is consistent across European tech markets. Data not available for Dublin-specific seniority breakdowns.

## Key Factors That Influence Data Scientist Pay in Dublin

Regardless of city, several factors consistently drive compensation variation for data scientists: industry sector (finance and pharma typically pay above the market median), company size and funding stage, the specific technical stack required (ML engineering skills command a premium over pure analytics roles), and whether the role is individual contributor or people management. In Dublin specifically, the concentration of multinational tech and financial services firms is a known driver of above-average compensation in data roles, though specific figures are not available in our current dataset.
